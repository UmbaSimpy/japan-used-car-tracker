ガイド  ·  Label Guide & FAQ

Every car is rated 0–10 from the factors below. Each factor gets a 0–10 sub-score, multiplied by a weight, and summed into the total; some lines also add flat accessory bonuses on top. Scores are colour-graded low → mid → high. Tap any item to expand.

📊 The score & its factors
Total score 8.4

The headline 0–10 number, shown top-right of each card and at the bottom of the breakdown box. It is the weighted sum of every factor below (plus any accessory bonus). Green ≥ 7.5, amber 5.5–7.4, red < 5.5. It’s a relative “value & condition” signal, not a guarantee — always read the individual factors, because two cars with the same total can be strong in very different areas.

Price weight 34–42%

What it is: how cheap the car is relative to others of the same kind. Every price here is the total out-the-door price (支払総額) — it includes fees, taxes and registration, not just the vehicle body price (車両本体価格) — because the bundled fees vary a lot between listings and the total is the truly comparable cost. The cheapest car scores 10 and the score falls off steeply, so a 10 is effectively reserved for the lowest price; most cars sit mid-to-low. For most models price is judged within each grade; for the StepWGN all grades share one pool (the cheapest StepWGN overall = 10).
Why it matters: it’s the single biggest factor (34–42% of the score) because price is what you actually pay. But a very high price score often means high mileage, no warranty, or short/expired shaken — a “cheap” car can cost more later. Always cross-check the cheap ones against mileage and condition.

Mileage 🛣 weight 12%

What it is: lower kilometres score higher (≈ 0 km → 10, 100,000 km → 0). Shown as the 🛣 pill.
Why it matters: lower mileage usually means less wear. But watch the trap: a near-0 km “登録済未使用車” (registered-unused) car scores a perfect mileage 10 yet is priced like new — not a bargain. That’s why mileage is deliberately weighted modestly. A 20–50k km car with a good price and full kit is often better value than a 5 km one at full retail.

Screen weight 18%

What it is: whether a head-unit / display is fitted — either a real navigation unit or a display-audio screen (ディスプレイオーディオ). Installed = 10; confirmed missing or unclear = 0. Cars advertised as ナビレス/オーディオレス, or that only carry the “nav-ready package” (ナビ装着用スペシャルパッケージ — wiring only, no unit), score 0.
Why it matters: retro-fitting a screen/nav is expensive and fiddly, and a missing one is a classic way dealers hit a low sticker price on near-new cars. Heavily weighted so those stripped units don’t look like bargains.

Accident No Accident

What it is: the repair-history flag (修復歴). No Accident = no structural repair history (best), Accident = has repair history, Acc N/A = not stated. Weight 13%.
Why it matters: in Japan 修復歴 specifically means frame/structural damage was repaired — it hurts resale value and can hide alignment or safety issues. For a family minivan most buyers want No Accident. A repaired car can be fine and cheaper, but only with a trusted inspection.

Warranty 保証付

What it is: whether a dealer warranty is included. 保証付 = warranty included, 保証なし = none, 保証 N/A = not stated. Weight 8%.
Why it matters: a warranty covers you if something fails soon after purchase. Valuable on a used car, but secondary to price/condition — and the length/scope varies a lot, so confirm the details with the dealer.

Maintenance 法定整備付

What it is: whether the legally-required pre-delivery servicing (法定整備) is included. 法定整備付 = included, 法定整備無 = not included, 整備 N/A = not stated. Weight 3%.
Why it matters: “付” means the car is serviced/checked before handover — peace of mind, and any cost is already in the price. “無” means you may pay for that servicing on top. Low weight because it’s a relatively small, one-off amount.

車検 Shaken 🔧 weight 4%

What it is: how many months of valid Japanese statutory inspection (車検) remain. 0 months → low, ≥ 24 months → high. Shown as the 🔧 pill.
Why it matters: a fresh 車検 saves you an inspection (often ¥100,000+) soon after buying. But it’s deliberately low-weighted: cars with little/no shaken are usually priced lower to compensate, and you can renew it yourself — so “車検なし” can still be a great buy (see the Hidden Gems category).

Multi-cam Freed only · weight 8%

What it is: a multi-view / surround-view camera (マルチビューカメラ / 全周囲 / パノラミックビュー) — not a plain reversing camera. On the Freed it’s still a scored factor; on the Noah, Voxy and StepWGN it’s instead an accessory tag (see below).
Why it matters: genuinely useful for parking a big minivan in tight Japanese spaces, and a factory option that’s costly to add later. Note: a listing’s “360°画像付” badge is just CarSensor’s photo viewer and is deliberately ignored.

🪑 Spec pills & accessory tags
Seats 🪑 7名

What it is: seating capacity from the detail page. Green = the preferred layout for that model (e.g. 6-seat captain chairs on the Freed, 7-seat on the Noah/Voxy/StepWGN); red = a less-wanted count (e.g. 8-seat bench). Cars with the un-preferred count are filtered out of the top list entirely.
Why it matters: the seat layout changes the whole interior (captain chairs vs bench, walk-through, luggage space) and is impossible to change later — for many buyers it’s a hard requirement.

Colour band e.g. ⬛ 黒

What it is: the body colour from the listing, shown as a swatch + name. Not scored.
Why it matters: purely personal preference / resale taste — white & black hold value best in Japan. Informational only; it never affects the score.

Accessory tags ✦ マルチビュー

What they are: desirable factory option packages, shown as purple tags and added as a small cumulative bonus (they stack) that appears as the Equip row in the breakdown:

• マルチビュー — multi-view/surround camera (StepWGN: the one worthwhile add-on, +0.30).
• 快適利便PKG (Noah/Voxy) — the Comfort & Convenience package (heated seats, extra USB/power, etc.), +0.15.
• Adv.ドライブ — Toyota Teammate Advanced Drive (hands-off highway assist), +0.30.
• Adv.パーク+PVM — Advanced Park (automated parking) bundled with the Panoramic View Monitor, +0.25. A car with this does not also get the standalone PVM bonus (no double-counting).
• PVM (Noah/Voxy) — standalone Panoramic View Monitor, +0.15.

Why they matter: these are pricey factory options that can’t be added afterwards, so two otherwise-identical cars aren’t equal if one has them. The bonus is deliberately small — a nice tiebreaker, not something that should override price/condition.

🏷️ Quick-spot badges
NEW  Newly listed

The car wasn’t on this board in the previous daily snapshot — i.e. a fresh listing. Worth a look because good new listings sell fast; it says nothing about quality.

¥ BEST  Lowest price

The cheapest car in that section (Best Overall, or a given Hidden-Gems category). A quick flag for the absolute price floor — but check why it’s cheapest (mileage, shaken, condition) before assuming it’s the best deal.

LOW KM  Lowest mileage

The lowest-mileage car in that section. Great for longevity, but remember the near-0 km cars are often priced like new — low km alone isn’t value.

Cost per km · Dealer rating ★ 4.6

Cost per km (¥/km) = price ÷ mileage — a rough “how much you’re paying per kilometre of life used.” Mainly useful for comparing higher-mileage cars; near-new cars show a huge ¥/km simply because the denominator is tiny.
Dealer rating = CarSensor’s buyer review score (★ out of 5) and review count. A higher score with many reviews is reassuring; it doesn’t affect the car’s score.

3M / 6M / 1Y & the price chart

The period buttons set how far back the price-history chart and trend comments look. The chart tracks each grade’s average listed price over time — useful for spotting whether prices are rising or softening before you commit.

Why don’t flood-damaged cars appear?

Listings flagged as flood-damaged (冠水歴車 / 水没車) are removed entirely — they’re excluded from the rankings and even from the price statistics, because flood history is a serious long-term reliability risk. Cars explicitly stating 冠水歴なし (no flood history) are kept.
